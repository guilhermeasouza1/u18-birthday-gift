02 · Proposed Solution

Birthday Gift — PIX Surprise

🎁
Option 3 · AI-First · Flow 2

The parent sends a special PIX that the child opens as a surprise in-app.

On D0 (child's birthday), legal guardians receive a notification offering them to send a PIX gift. The minor then gets a surprise notification and a celebratory screen. Two treatments are tested against a control — to understand whether emotional experience or simple reminders drive more transfers.

👨‍👧 Legal Guardians 👤 U18 Minors ✦ AI-First

Targeting rules

→Multiple minors: 1 notification per minor per LG on each child's birthday
→Multiple LGs: All active legal guardians of a minor are notified
→Dynamic copy: {{nome}} → u18_customer_first_name

Key test distinction

T1 — tests whether emotional experience (gift card + minor surprise) drives higher transfers
T2 — tests whether a simple reminder alone is sufficient to trigger transfers
💡 If T2 ≥ T1, occasion awareness alone drives behavior — emotional packaging is not the key driver.

05 · Risks & Compliance

Known risks requiring resolution

High
Medium
Legal / Compliance
LGPD — Secondary use of minor's DOB as commercial trigger

Using the minor's date of birth to trigger commercial comms may exceed original consent scope (LGPD Art. 6 — purpose limitation; Art. 14 — minor's best interest). Requires legal sign-off from @julia.dandara.

Product / Ops
Marketing opt-out suppression distorts conversion metrics

If classified as :promotion, push is suppressed for opted-out LGs, biasing experiment results. Action: verify notification category (transactional preferred); monitor opt-out rate.

Privacy
Minor's first name on device lock screen = PII exposure

Lock-screen display of the minor's name is PII outside the secured app environment (LGPD Art. 14; U18 PII ACG policy). Mitigation: truncate to "Feliz aniversário! 🎂" without the name. Validation required.
